About Me.

I am an architect of intelligence.

My journey began with a simple curiosity: How can we make machines see? This question led me down the rabbit hole of Computer Vision and Deep Learning. Today, I stand at the intersection of Healthcare and Artificial Intelligence, building systems that don't just process data, but understand it to save lives.

Currently, I am focused on Medical Imaging (DICOM) at Arthro3D. My day-to-day involves wrestling with 3D voxel data, designing custom U-Net architectures for semantic segmentation, and optimizing inference pipelines for real-time surgical planning.

But I'm not just about the algorithms. I believe in the Zen of Python. Code should be clean, explicitly typed, and built to scale. Whether it's a microservice in FastAPI or a complex C++ mesh processing module, I engineer for reliability.
